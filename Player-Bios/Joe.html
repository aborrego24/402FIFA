Tournament Performances:

1st annual World Cup

Joe came into the tournament on a 5 game losing streak leading up to the match against his rival: Michael

He ended up winning an offensive showcase from both players, with the game totaling 22 shots.

He then went on to lose his next two games to the eventual runner-up Gabe, and Karsin in the loser's semi-finals.

Chicago Invitational

With no access to an Xbox over the summer, Joe came into the tournament facing his toughest battle yet, as his two rivals, Michael and Alejandro, had been practicing all summer. He would narrowly escape his first game against JT, winning 1 - 0. His next game would prove difficult, as Michael was out for revenge after a defeat to Joe in the World Cup that saw Michael score 0 goals on 16 shots. This time, Michael capitalized on his opportunities, smashing 3 goals past Joe's defense. It also didn't help that Joe picked up 2 Red Cards, but he did score 2 penalties. The final scoreline of 3 - 2 put the league on notice, and this rivalry is only getting more intense.
